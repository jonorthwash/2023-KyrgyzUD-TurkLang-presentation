Overview

Our talk in a nutshell

We've been working on UD annotation of Kyrgyz material, mostly proverbs and literature.

The next ~13 minutes:

  • Background on corpus
  • Pros and cons of genres
  • Annotation issues

Background on corpus

  • 2020: Anna puts together 86-sentence proverbs corpus, with supervision from Jonathan
    • Used Apertium (apertium-kir) to pre-process sentences (tokenisation, morphological analysis)
  • 2021: Aida starts working with students on proverbs, literature, and novel sentences
  • 2023: current state:
    • sentences: ≥158
    • tokens: ≥1124
    • Ongoing efforts to combine, check, unify annotations, add metadata, and expand
  • Future: submit as one corpus? Or divide by genre, annotator, ..?

Genres: pros and cons

Cons

  • Mostly short, declarative sentences
    • Missing a wide range of structures, morphology, etc.
  • Very literary language:
    • Words uncommon in everyday language
    • Structures not typically found in speech

Pros

  • Interesting to those who study folklore, literature, anthropology
  • Public domain*
  • Otherwise uncommon structures encountered:

Challenges: copula tokenisation

Defective copula verb э-

  • Irregular attested forms:
    • non-past tense: endings on predicate (үйдөмүн, not үйдө эймин)
    • direct past tense: эле (not эди) + past-tense person endings
    • irregular-looking past verbal noun: экен (not эген)
  • other words derived from э-: эмес not, экен evidently
  • Missing other forms:
    • tenses, non-finite forms: эчү, эген, өө, эш, ээр, эп, эй, etc.
    • negative forms: эбеле
  • The problem: tokenise present and past in parallel way
  • A solution: supertokens

Substantivised verbal adjectives

  • Verbal adjectives with "empty head"
  • Can be read in English as "the person/thing/one"
  • Two options:
    • Treat simply as "clause": ccomp, csubj
    • Treat as noun (not present): obj, nsubj (✔?)
