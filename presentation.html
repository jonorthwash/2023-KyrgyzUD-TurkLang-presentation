Challenges: copula tokenisation

Defective copula verb э-

  • Irregular attested forms:
    • non-past tense: endings on predicate (үйдөмүн, not үйдө эймин)
    • direct past tense: эле (not эди) + past-tense person endings
    • irregular-looking past verbal noun: экен (not эген)
  • other words derived from э-: эмес not, экен evidently
  • Missing other forms:
    • tenses, non-finite forms: эчү, эген, өө, эш, ээр, эп, эй, etc.
    • negative forms: эбеле

Other UD corpora

  • Kazakh (Tyers & Washington 2015)
    • Tokenise copula separately (like we do)
    • Except not for 3rd person (when null)
  • Kyrgyz (Benli 2023)
    • no separate tokenisation
    • predicates with copula affixes sometimes analysed as verbs
    • эле analysed as VERB and compound:svc dependent of non-verbal predicate
  • The problem: tokenise present and past in parallel way
  • A solution: supertokens

Challenges: small words: да

Uses of да

  1. Post-predicate "modal particle"
    Натыйжалар жарыяланыптыр да, ээ?
    ‘The results have been announced, haven't they?’
  2. Conditional intensifier
    Оозу кыйшык болсо да, байдын уулу сүйлөсүн.
    ‘Even if his mouth is crooked, let the rich person's son speak.’
  3. General contrastive intensifier
    Тамашада да чындыктын үлүшү бар.
    ‘Even in a joke is some element of truth.’
  4. General conjoining adverb
    Атам да каршы болду.
    ‘My father was also against it.’ (or: ‘Even my father was against it.’)
  5. Correlative conjunction
    Атам да, апам да каршы болду.
    ‘Both my father and my mother were against it.’

Uses 2&3, 3&4, 4&5 seem very similar: we treat the same

Challenges: substantivised verbal adjectives

  • Verbal adjectives with "empty head"
  • Can be read in English as "the person/thing/one"
  • Two options:
    • Treat simply as "clause": ccomp, csubj
    • Treat as noun (not present): obj, nsubj (✔)
